Three-Story Thesis

A strong thesis has three stories: the inarguable facts of the situation, an arguable point those facts make possible, and the broader implications that make the argument matter. Build yours layer by layer.

Story 1 — The Facts
State the inarguable context your argument rests on. What does every reader need to know before you can make your claim?
Story 2 — The Arguable Point
What is your interpretive or analytical claim — non-obvious, worth arguing, something a reasonable reader might dispute?
Story 3 — The Implications
Why does your argument matter beyond the immediate topic? What larger questions, stakes, or conversations does it enter?
